What to do about Evil Martians
Professional, not emotional. You don't need to burn bridges — you need to stop investing emotional energy where it won't return value.
  • Reduce discretionary effort. Do exactly what's scoped. Stop working until midnight on unscoped polish.
  • Document everything. Your presentations, client feedback, shipped features. This is your portfolio raw material.
  • Set conditions. "I can deliver Mercedes quality with Mercedes timelines, or fast iteration quality with 1-2 day timelines. Which do you want?"
  • Use bench time strategically. This is when you build your brand and portfolio assets.
  • Ask for the review decision in writing. "The feedback collection finished March 3. Can you share the timeline for the review decision?"
  • Baby news: In most jurisdictions, this provides additional employment protections. Research your specific rights.

90-Day Launchpad

A practical plan that works with a baby on the way. You don't need to quit tomorrow — you need to start building leverage today. Each phase has clear deliverables.

Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1-30)
Goal: Set conditions + start building in public
  • Set professional boundaries at current job. Do exactly what's scoped. Stop uncompensated overtime.
  • Request review decision in writing. "The feedback was collected March 3. What's the timeline?"
  • Calculate your number. Monthly expenses × 12 = savings target. Know exactly what you need.
  • Buy a domain. yourname.com or a memorable brand domain.
  • Launch personal site. Doesn't need to be complex. Who you help, what you do, proof. Use your playground skills — you can build this in 2 hours.
  • Start Substack newsletter. "Design Engineering with AI" or similar. Publish first 4 posts.
  • Post on X/Twitter daily. Build-in-public about your skills, playgrounds, workflow.
  • Package first case study. Pick your best project (oklch-color-lab or Agent Skills). Write it up with outcomes, not just process.
  • Research employment protections related to your family situation in your jurisdiction.
Phase 2: Positioning & Visibility (Days 31-60)
Goal: Get known + define your productized offering
  • Package second and third case studies. Design System Pipeline + client work (anonymized if needed).
  • Define productized service. "Design System Health Check — $8K, 2-week turnaround, prioritized report with actionable fixes." Create a landing page.
  • List first agent skill bundle. Choose your best 10-15 skills, package and price at $49.
  • Publish on established platforms. Pitch 1-2 guest posts (Smashing Magazine, CSS-Tricks, dev.to, or LogRocket blog).
  • Start LinkedIn presence. 2-3 posts/week. LinkedIn reaches decision-makers who hire consultants.
  • Have 5 "coffee chats" with independent consultants, agency owners, or CTOs in your network.
  • Apply to 3-5 design engineer roles at companies that value your skillset (Vercel, Linear, Stripe, funded startups).
  • Newsletter at 8+ posts. Share each one across all platforms.
Phase 3: Revenue & Decision (Days 61-90)
Goal: First money + clear next step
  • Close first paying engagement. Even if discounted. Revenue = validation.
  • Track: revenue, hours, effective rate, pipeline, content engagement.
  • If offers come from job applications: evaluate comp, equity, culture, and whether they see your full value.
  • If solo revenue is promising: Continue building. Set a "go independent" threshold (e.g., 50% of salary from side work).
  • Start documenting your process for eventual course creation.
  • Newsletter at 12+ posts. Consistent weekly publishing builds compound trust.
  • Evaluate: Continue at EM? New role? Go independent? You'll have data to decide.
  • Start planning for baby arrival. Build buffer in schedule and savings.
Non-Negotiable Principles
Do
  • Compete on specificity and depth, never on price
  • Own your distribution — email list, personal site, direct relationships
  • Price on value — 10% of value created, not hourly time
  • Build in public — transparency builds trust faster than polish
  • Position around judgment, not execution — that's the AI-proof moat
Don't
  • Don't quit without savings. 9-12 months with a baby, minimum.
  • Don't burn bridges. Professional distance, not emotional exit.
  • Don't skip the audience step. Courses and products need buyers first.
  • Don't compete as a generalist. "I do design and code" is commoditized.
  • Don't depend on one platform. Tailwind lost 80% revenue when AI changed traffic patterns.

The Meta-Pattern
Every success story follows the same sequence:

1. Build something genuinely useful while employed. Don't monetize yet. Focus on quality.
2. Develop audience through contributions. Write, speak, share freely.
3. When reputation creates inbound demand that conflicts with the day job → go independent.
4. Layer revenue: consulting (immediate) → productized services (scalable) → digital products (passive).

You are between step 1 and step 2. The building is done — 99 skills, 15+ playgrounds, deep research. Now you need to make it visible. That's the newsletter, the personal site, the social presence. The audience comes because the work is good — and your work is already good.
